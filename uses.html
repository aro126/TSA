FUTURE USES:

From Ivan Suthernland's head-mounted display system in 1968 to Google Glass first released to the public in 2014, augmented reality technology has advanced and become more available for public use. Recent advancements and innovations point to a promising future for AR tech to become more accessible in our lives.

The ability to visualize 3D images as if they were real objects is attracting entrepreneurs and many large technology companies (ft.com). Tim Cook, Apple chief executive, has said AR would become an “everyday” experience for consumers. Apple has been experimenting with headset prototypes in its labs over the past year. Several companies are taking advantage of the latest advances in image processing and lower energy consumption from chipmakers such as Intel, Qualcomm and Nvidia to further the development of their headsets (ft.com).
